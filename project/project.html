CLOUD PVR / NPVR

Date:May 28,2017
Whats the opportunity

There are plenty of Set Top Boxes (STBs) which do not have a built in storage. Even those STBs which have storage have limitations of the number of hours of recording. With HD and 4K content the storage requirments is balooning. Therefore, STBs with PVRs require frequent upkeep by deleting old programs.

Current Scenario

  • All content recoded may not be watched but consumes bandwidth and storage space.
  • Requires significant pre-planing for recording content.
  • Network outages, cloudy weather interuptions in DTH results in poor quality content re-corded.
  • PVR boxes are expensive resulting is low penetration
  • Storage hardware wears out over time and needs very expensive re-furbishing.

The solution

Cloud PVR or NPVR ensures all the content is stored on the cloud or at the broadcasters server. Broadcaster doesn’t need to store the same content repeatedly. Each recoding request needs to be just ear marked.

Customer Benefits

  • Now even Low cost STBs have PVR functionality.
  • All existing customer base has premium PVR facility.
  • Advertisers reach increases over stored content use case aswell.

Consumer Value
  • No more expensive STBs
  • Enhanced user experience due to ease of maintaining the recorded content.
  • Reduces bandwith consumtion
  • Last mile bandwidth put to better use.
  • User’s cloud subscription can be leveraged.
  • A green solution .

HEALTHCARE ANALYTICS FOR INSURANCE

Date:May 28,2017

Healthcare Insurance industry stands a lot to gain from using adaptive, and predictive analytical systems to make better use of the large demographic health data from across the globe. Instead of forcefully bundling various people into loosely fitting buckets for statistical analysis, insurance companies benefit better by personalizing their models for individuals.

The solution is to use big data analytical platforms, using self assembling statistical models based on key patient information. Traditional methods involve using a client’s personal parameters to arrive at a certain values that are fed into risk model that predict illness and death, among others. However, the availability of large data sets as well as advancements in computational actuarial sciences enable us to analyze thousands of risk parameters either separately or in smaller buckets, in the context of the client’s individuality. They can then be put through several actuarial models to arrive at a better suited and relatively fair model from the client’s point of view.
